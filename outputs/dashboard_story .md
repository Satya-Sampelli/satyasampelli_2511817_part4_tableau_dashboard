## Executive Summary

The executive dashboard presents a comprehensive view of retail sales performance across regions, product categories, customer segments, and operational metrics for 2024–2025. Total sales stand at ₹217 million with a profit of ₹33.3 million, representing a 15.35% overall profit margin.

The headline story is one of strong Technology-led growth concentrated in the South region, offset by concerning return rates in Furniture, a negative relationship between discounting and profitability, and relatively slow Standard Class delivery performance.

---

## What is Performing Well

**Technology is the engine of the business.** At ₹153.9 million in sales and ₹28 million in profit, Technology contributes over 70% of total revenue and 84% of total profit. Sub-categories like Copiers (₹7.31M profit), Accessories (₹7.19M), and Phones (₹7.10M) are the strongest profit drivers in the portfolio.

**South region leads the market.** With ₹64.7 million in sales and ₹9.99 million in profit, South outperforms every other region. This suggests effective market penetration, strong customer relationships, or a favourable store and product mix in the region.

**Customer segments are balanced and healthy.** All three segments — Home Office (₹74.5M), Consumer (₹71.9M) and Corporate (₹70.6M) — contribute relatively equally to total revenue, reducing dependence on any single customer type.

**Same Day and First Class shipping are fast.** Average delivery of 0.4 days for Same Day and 1.77 days for First Class shows that the logistics infrastructure can deliver quickly when required.

---

## What is Underperforming

**Office Supplies is a weak category.** Despite occupying shelf space and requiring inventory management, Office Supplies generates only ₹11.5M in sales and ₹1.7M in profit — less than 8% of total profit. Sub-categories like Paper (₹0.32M profit), Binders (₹0.33M) and Storage (₹0.34M) are particularly low-value.

**East and West regions lag behind South and North.** Both East (₹48.9M) and West (₹48.9M) are significantly behind South (₹64.7M). The gap between South and East represents approximately ₹15.8M in untapped sales potential if East could match South's performance.

**Standard Class delivery is slow.** At an average of 4.71 days, Standard Class — likely the most commonly used shipping method — delivers far slower than other modes. This could be contributing to customer dissatisfaction.

---

## What Risks are Visible

**Heavy Technology dependency is a concentration risk.** With 70.9% of sales coming from a single category, any supply disruption, competitor entry, or pricing pressure in Technology would have an outsized negative impact on total business performance.

**Furniture return rate of 7.67% is a serious operational concern.** Returns are expensive — they involve logistics costs, restocking effort, and lost margin. A return rate more than double the Technology rate (3.03%) suggests systematic issues with Furniture product quality, packaging, or customer expectations.

**Discounting is eroding profitability.** The scatter plot shows a clear negative correlation (-0.27) between discount rate and profit. Orders with discounts above 25% are consistently low-profit or loss-making. Without a discount cap, this pattern will continue to compress margins.

**Home Office segment has the highest return rate (5.67%).** Since Home Office is also the highest-revenue segment, a high return rate here represents both a financial risk and a customer satisfaction risk.

---

## What Opportunities are Visible

**Replicating South region success in East and West** could add approximately ₹31.6M in additional annual sales if both regions matched South's performance level.

**Growing Furniture profitability** by reducing returns through better product quality or packaging could meaningfully improve overall margins, given Furniture's ₹51.6M in sales.

**Email and Paid channels are underpenetrated.** Organic drives ₹88.8M in sales but represents revenue that the business cannot fully control or scale. Paid (₹40.1M) and Email (₹34.5M) channels have clear room for growth through targeted investment.

**Corporate segment margin improvement** — Corporate has the lowest profit (₹10.7M) despite similar sales to the other segments. Tightening corporate discount policy could meaningfully improve profitability without losing significant volume.

---

## Recommended Business Actions

1. **Set a maximum discount cap of 20%** across all categories to protect profit margins and eliminate loss-making orders.
2. **Investigate and address Furniture return root causes** — whether product quality, packaging, or misleading descriptions — to bring the return rate below 5%.
3. **Replicate South region strategies in East and West** through targeted marketing investment and product mix optimisation.
4. **Diversify away from Technology concentration** by setting category mix targets and investing in high-margin Furniture and Office Supplies products.
5. **Improve Standard Class delivery time** by reviewing logistics partnerships and warehouse processes to achieve an average below 3.5 days.
6. **Increase Email marketing investment** targeting Home Office and Corporate segments to drive scalable, controllable revenue growth.

---

## Limitations of the Dashboard

- The dashboard covers 2024–2025 data only — longer historical trends would provide stronger seasonal insights.
- Customer lifetime value and repeat purchase behaviour are not captured — the data shows transaction-level performance but not customer loyalty metrics.
- Profitability at the product level is available but not fully explored in the current dashboard views.
- Campaign channel data has 24 missing values — some orders cannot be attributed to a specific channel, which may slightly understate certain channel contributions.
- Customer rating data has 32 missing values — the relationship between rating and sales or returns could be explored further.

---

## Suggested Next Analysis

1. **Customer retention analysis** — what percentage of customers make repeat purchases, and which segments have the highest loyalty?
2. **Seasonal trend deep dive** — are certain months consistently stronger or weaker, and can inventory and staffing be planned accordingly?
3. **Discount effectiveness study** — a controlled experiment to test whether reducing discounts from 25% to 20% retains volume or reduces it.
4. **Furniture return root cause analysis** — a product-level breakdown of which specific Furniture items have the highest return rates.
5. **Regional market share analysis** — compare company sales data against market size estimates to assess penetration rates by region.
